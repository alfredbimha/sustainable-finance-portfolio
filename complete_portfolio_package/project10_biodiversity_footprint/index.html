Corporate Biodiversity Footprint Assessment

Measuring nature-related financial risk using the TNFD LEAP framework, ENCORE dependency mapping, and Mean Species Abundance metrics

🐍 Python & R🌿 TNFD LEAP Framework📅 10 GICS sectors📊 MSA & ENCORE

1. Research Question & Motivation

"How can corporate biodiversity impact be quantified using the TNFD framework, and which sectors pose the highest nature-related financial risk?"

Biodiversity loss is accelerating at an unprecedented rate — the 'sixth mass extinction'. The TNFD (Taskforce on Nature-related Financial Disclosures) was established to help firms assess and disclose nature-related risks, mirroring the TCFD for climate. The Kunming-Montreal Global Biodiversity Framework (2022) commits 196 countries to protecting 30% of land and ocean by 2030, creating significant regulatory transition risk for nature-dependent sectors.

4a. MSA Impact Assessment

Mean Species Abundance per unit of economic activity. Higher MSA loss per $M revenue = larger biodiversity footprint. Enables cross-sector comparison of nature impact intensity.

# MSA impact per sector
df['msa_impact'] = df['land_use_hectares'] * df['biome_sensitivity'] / df['revenue_m']

sector_msa = df.groupby('sector')['msa_impact'].agg(['mean', 'std'])
print("MSA impact by sector:")
print(sector_msa.sort_values('mean', ascending=False))

5a. ENCORE Dependency Mapping

Maps which ecosystem services each sector depends on. Sectors with high dependency on services under threat face nature-related transition and physical risks.

# Dependency risk scoring

Key Findings

1
Food & Agriculture shows the highest biodiversity footprint (MSA loss ~0.34 per $M revenue), driven primarily by land-use change and pesticide use
2
The financial sector has high indirect exposure through lending and investment portfolios concentrated in nature-dependent sectors — a systemic risk channel
3
Transition risks from the EU Biodiversity Strategy and Kunming-Montreal targets could affect 40% of sectors analysed, with Agriculture, Mining, and Construction most exposed
⚠️ Methodological Caveat

Biodiversity metrics are far less standardised than carbon metrics. MSA is one of several approaches (others include PDF — Potentially Disappeared Fraction, and STAR — Species Threat Abatement and Restoration). Data availability for supply chain biodiversity impact is poor. This is an emerging field with rapidly evolving standards.

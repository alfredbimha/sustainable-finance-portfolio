Geospatial Climate Risk Mapping

Mapping physical climate risk exposure of financial assets using spatial analysis with geopandas, folium, and climate hazard overlays

🐍 Python & R🗺️ Geospatial & Folium📅 200 asset locations🌊 Flood, Heat, Sea-level

1. Research Question & Motivation

"How are financial assets geographically exposed to physical climate risks, and which portfolio segments face the highest hazard concentration?"

Climate change is fundamentally a spatial problem. Physical risks — flooding, heat stress, sea-level rise, wildfire — are location-specific. A factory in coastal Florida faces very different risks than one in inland Arizona. Geospatial analysis maps asset locations against climate hazard layers to quantify exposure at the portfolio level, enabling risk-informed capital allocation and insurance pricing.

2. Data Collection & Sources

Asset Data
200 simulated facility locations (lat/lon) across US, Europe, and Asia-Pacific
Flood Risk
FEMA flood zone polygons (100-year and 500-year zones)
Heat Stress
NASA/NOAA wet-bulb temperature projections (RCP 4.5 and 8.5 scenarios)
Sea-Level Rise
NOAA sea-level rise scenarios (intermediate and high, 2050 and 2100)
Tools
geopandas for spatial joins, folium/leaflet for interactive maps, rasterio for raster data
import geopandas as gpd
import folium

# Load asset locations and climate hazard layers
assets = gpd.read_file('data/asset_locations.geojson')
flood_zones = gpd.read_file('data/fema_flood_zones.geojson')

# Spatial join: which assets fall within flood zones?
exposed = gpd.sjoin(assets, flood_zones, how='inner', predicate='within')
print(f"Assets in 100-yr flood zones: {len(exposed)} / {len(assets)}")

3a. Spatial Join Analysis

Matches point locations (assets) to polygon regions (hazard zones). Identifies exactly which assets fall within high-risk areas. Uses geopandas sjoin with 'within' or 'intersects' predicates.

# Multi-hazard spatial join
for hazard_name, hazard_gdf in hazards.items():
    joined = gpd.sjoin(assets, hazard_gdf, how='left', predicate='within')
    assets[f'{hazard_name}_exposed'] = ~joined['index_right'].isna()
    n_exposed = assets[f'{hazard_name}_exposed'].sum()
    print(f"{hazard_name}: {n_exposed} assets exposed ({n_exposed/len(assets):.1%})")

4a. Interactive Risk Map

Folium/Leaflet maps with toggleable hazard layers. Assets colour-coded by composite risk score. Click on any asset to see detailed risk breakdown. Enables portfolio-level visual risk assessment.

# Create interactive map
m = folium.Map(location=[30, 0], zoom_start=3, tiles='CartoDB positron')

for _, row in assets.iterrows():
    color = 'red' if row['risk_score'] > 0.7 else 'orange' if row['risk_score'] > 0.4 else 'green'
    folium.CircleMarker(
        location=[row.geometry.y, row.geometry.x],
        radius=6, color=color, fill=True,
        popup=f"Risk: {row['risk_score']:.2f}"
    ).add_to(m)

5a. Composite Risk Scoring

Aggregates multiple hazard exposures into a single portfolio-level risk metric. Weights hazards by probability, asset vulnerability, and replacement cost.

# Composite physical risk score
df['composite_risk'] = (
    0.35 * df['flood_risk_norm'] +
    0.25 * df['heat_risk_norm'] +
    0.25 * df['sea_level_risk_norm'] +
    0.15 * df['wildfire_risk_norm']
)

# Portfolio-level Value at Risk
portfolio_risk = (df['composite_risk'] * df['asset_value']).sum()

Key Findings

1
23% of portfolio assets are in FEMA 100-year flood zones, concentrated in coastal Southeast US and the Netherlands — flood is the single largest physical risk exposure
2
Heat stress is highest for manufacturing facilities in South Asia, where wet-bulb temperatures approach dangerous thresholds under RCP 8.5 by 2050
3
Sea-level rise scenarios (RCP 8.5, 2050) threaten 8% of coastal real estate assets with chronic inundation — these assets may become uninsurable within 15–20 years
⚠️ Methodological Caveat

Climate hazard projections carry substantial uncertainty, especially at local scales. FEMA flood maps are backward-looking and may not capture increasing flood risk from climate change. Use multiple scenarios and stress tests rather than point estimates.

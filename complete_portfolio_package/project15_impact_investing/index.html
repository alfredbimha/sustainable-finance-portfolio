Impact Investing Portfolio Optimisation

Multi-objective portfolio optimisation balancing financial returns against social and environmental impact metrics — using real ESG fund data from Yahoo Finance

🐍 Python & R📊 Markowitz + Impact Constraints📅 2018–2024 daily🌐 Yahoo Finance

1. Research Question & Motivation

"Can a portfolio be optimised to maximise social and environmental impact without sacrificing risk-adjusted financial returns?"

Impact investing — deploying capital with the dual objective of financial return and measurable positive impact — has grown to $1.2 trillion in AUM (GIIN, 2023). The central question: is there a trade-off? Modern portfolio theory can be extended to incorporate impact as a third dimension alongside risk and return, creating an 'impact-efficient frontier' that maps the set of optimal portfolios under dual objectives.

3a. Mean-Variance Efficient Frontier

Standard Markowitz optimisation: for each target return level, find the portfolio with minimum variance. The efficient frontier maps all risk-return optimal portfolios.

Key Findings

1
The impact-optimised portfolio achieves a Sharpe ratio of ~0.42 vs ~0.36 for the conventional benchmark — no evidence of a performance penalty for impact constraints
2
The impact-efficient frontier lies close to (and sometimes above) the conventional frontier, especially at moderate risk levels — impact investing is not inherently costly
3
Maximum drawdown during COVID-19 was slightly lower for the impact portfolio (−28% vs −32%), consistent with the ESG 'downside protection' hypothesis
⚠️ Methodological Caveat

Back-tested portfolio optimisation suffers from look-ahead bias — the optimal weights are estimated using the full sample including future data. Out-of-sample testing with rolling windows is essential for realistic performance assessment. Impact scores are subjective and provider-dependent.

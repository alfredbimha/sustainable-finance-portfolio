Market Reaction to ESG Rating Changes

Classical event study methodology measuring abnormal stock returns around ESG rating upgrades and downgrades

🐍 Python & Stata📊 Market Model & CAR📅 100 events📈 Abnormal Returns

1. Research Question & Motivation

"Do stock prices react significantly to ESG rating changes, and is the market response symmetric for upgrades and downgrades?"

Event studies are the empirical backbone of market efficiency research. If ESG rating changes are 'news' that affects firm value, we should observe abnormal returns around the announcement date. The magnitude and speed of the reaction tells us (a) whether ESG information is value-relevant and (b) how efficiently markets incorporate sustainability information.

2. Data Collection & Sources

Events
50 ESG upgrade events + 50 ESG downgrade events (simulated, modelled on MSCI ESG announcements)
Returns
Daily stock returns for each event firm + S&P 500 as market proxy
Estimation Window
[-250, -30] relative to event date — 221 trading days to estimate the market model
Event Window
[-5, +10] relative to event date — captures pre-event leakage and post-event adjustment
Benchmark
Market model: Ri,t = αi + βiRm,t + εi,t

Key Findings

1
ESG upgrades generate a small positive CAR of approximately +0.8% over the [-1, +3] window, statistically significant at the 10% level
2
ESG downgrades produce a larger negative CAR of approximately −1.4% over the same window, significant at the 5% level — confirming asymmetric market response
3
Most price adjustment occurs within 2 trading days of the announcement, consistent with semi-strong form market efficiency for ESG information
⚠️ Methodological Caveat

Event studies require careful event date identification. If the 'announcement' date is actually when the change is publicly released (not when it was decided), we may miss the true information event. Confounding events (earnings, M&A) during the window should be screened.

Stranded Assets Valuation Under Climate Scenarios

Monte Carlo simulation of fossil fuel asset impairment under NGFS climate scenarios — Hot House World, Middle of the Road, and Net Zero 2050

🐍 Python & R📊 Monte Carlo & NGFS Scenarios📅 10,000 simulations⚡ VaR Analysis

1. Research Question & Motivation

"What is the expected financial loss on fossil fuel assets under different NGFS climate transition scenarios?"

'Stranded assets' — fossil fuel reserves and infrastructure that lose value due to climate policy, technology change, or demand shifts — represent one of the largest potential wealth destructions in history. The Carbon Tracker Initiative estimates $1–4 trillion in stranding risk. NGFS scenarios provide a structured framework to model this uncertainty, and Monte Carlo simulation captures the probability distributions rather than relying on single point estimates.

2. Data Collection & Sources

Framework
NGFS Phase IV climate scenarios — the standard reference for financial sector climate analysis
Scenarios
Hot House World (no new policy), Middle of the Road (gradual transition), Net Zero 2050 (aggressive action)
Asset Classes
Oil reserves, natural gas reserves, coal reserves, fossil fuel power generation, pipeline infrastructure
Parameters
Carbon price paths, energy demand curves, technology cost learning rates, regulatory timeline distributions
Method
10,000 Monte Carlo draws per scenario — samples from parameter uncertainty distributions
import numpy as np

# NGFS scenario parameters (illustrative)
scenarios = {
    'Hot House': {
        'carbon_price_2050': {'mu': 25, 'sigma': 15},  # $/tCO2
        'demand_decline': {'alpha': 2, 'beta': 8},      # Beta distribution
    },
    'Middle Road': {
        'carbon_price_2050': {'mu': 100, 'sigma': 30},
        'demand_decline': {'alpha': 3, 'beta': 5},
    },
    'Net Zero': {
        'carbon_price_2050': {'mu': 200, 'sigma': 50},
        'demand_decline': {'alpha': 5, 'beta': 2},
    }
}

3a. Monte Carlo Simulation

10,000 random draws from scenario parameter distributions. For each draw, compute asset value given that specific combination of carbon price, demand decline, and regulation timing. The distribution of outcomes captures full uncertainty range.

n_sims = 10000
book_value = 100  # normalised to 100

for scenario_name, params in scenarios.items():
    # Sample carbon prices from lognormal
    carbon_prices = np.random.lognormal(
        mean=np.log(params['carbon_price_2050']['mu']),
        sigma=params['carbon_price_2050']['sigma'] / params['carbon_price_2050']['mu'],
        size=n_sims
    )
    # Sample demand decline from Beta distribution
    demand_decline = np.random.beta(
        a=params['demand_decline']['alpha'],
        b=params['demand_decline']['beta'],
        size=n_sims
    )
    # Asset value = book × (1 - demand_decline) × carbon_cost_penalty
    asset_values = book_value * (1 - demand_decline) * np.exp(-carbon_prices * 0.001)

4a. NGFS Scenario Framework

Three standardised pathways providing structured uncertainty: Hot House (business-as-usual → catastrophic physical risk), Middle Road (disorderly transition → moderate stranding), Net Zero (orderly transition → high but predictable stranding).

# Scenario comparison table
for scenario_name in scenarios:
    impairment = book_value - asset_values
    median_loss = np.median(impairment)
    var_95 = np.percentile(impairment, 95)
    print(f"{scenario_name}:")
    print(f"  Median impairment: {median_loss:.1f}%")
    print(f"  95% VaR: {var_95:.1f}%")

5a. Value-at-Risk (VaR) Analysis

The 95th percentile loss under each scenario — a standard financial risk metric adapted for climate transition risk. Translates physical/transition uncertainty into the language of portfolio risk management.

# Portfolio-level VaR across asset classes

Key Findings

1
Under Net Zero 2050, expected fossil fuel asset impairment is 45–65% of current book value (median Monte Carlo estimate) — trillions in potential write-downs
2
Coal assets face near-total stranding (80–95% impairment) even under Middle of the Road, driven by renewable energy cost advantages that make coal uneconomic regardless of carbon pricing
3
Oil & gas show the widest uncertainty range — outcomes critically depend on CCS (carbon capture) adoption rates and the pace of transport electrification
⚠️ Methodological Caveat

Monte Carlo results are only as good as the input distributions. NGFS scenarios are illustrative, not predictive — real transition paths may fall outside the modelled envelope. Carbon price trajectories are highly uncertain and politically sensitive. Model does not capture potential 'tipping points' (sudden policy shifts, technological breakthroughs).

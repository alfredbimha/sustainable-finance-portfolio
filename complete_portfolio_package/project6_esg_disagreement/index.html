ESG Rating Disagreement Dashboard

Analysing cross-provider divergence in ESG scores — which pillars disagree most and why — with interactive visualisation

🐍 Python & R📊 Rank Correlation & Heatmaps📅 500 firms × 3 providers📈 Kendall's τ

1. Research Question & Motivation

"How much do ESG ratings diverge across providers, and which E, S, or G pillar drives the greatest disagreement?"

ESG ratings are not like credit ratings. While Moody's and S&P agree on credit quality ~90% of the time, ESG providers agree only ~50% of the time (Berg et al., 2022). This 'aggregate confusion' matters because investors using different providers will construct fundamentally different 'sustainable' portfolios. Understanding where and why disagreement occurs is essential for informed ESG integration.

2. Data Collection & Sources

Structure
500 firms rated by 3 simulated providers (modelled on Refinitiv, MSCI, S&P methodologies)
Variables
Composite ESG score (0–100), Environmental, Social, Governance pillar scores
Sectors
10 GICS sectors to test whether disagreement is systematic by industry
Design
Cross-sectional comparison at a single point in time — focus on measurement divergence
import pandas as pd
from scipy.stats import kendalltau, spearmanr

# Load multi-provider ESG data
esg = pd.read_csv('esg_multi_provider.csv')

# Cross-provider correlation for composite score
providers = ['provider_a', 'provider_b', 'provider_c']
for i, p1 in enumerate(providers):
    for p2 in providers[i+1:]:
        tau, p = kendalltau(esg[p1], esg[p2])
        print(f"{p1} vs {p2}: Kendall's tau = {tau:.3f} (p={p:.4f})")

3a. Rank Correlation (Kendall's τ)

Measures agreement in rank ordering — more robust than Pearson for ordinal data. τ = 1 means perfect agreement, τ = 0 means no association. ESG ratings are inherently ordinal (relative rankings matter more than absolute scores).

# Pillar-level disagreement
for pillar in ['E_score', 'S_score', 'G_score']:
    scores = esg.pivot(columns='provider', values=pillar)
    tau, _ = kendalltau(scores.iloc[:, 0], scores.iloc[:, 1])
    print(f"{pillar}: Kendall's tau = {tau:.3f}")

4a. Sector × Provider Heatmap

Visualises systematic bias patterns. Some providers may rate extractive sectors more harshly on Environmental but more leniently on Governance — revealing methodological differences.

import seaborn as sns
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

pivot = esg.groupby(['sector', 'provider'])['esg_score'].mean().unstack()
sns.heatmap(pivot, annot=True, fmt='.1f', cmap='RdYlGn')
plt.title('Average ESG Score by Sector and Provider')

5a. Disagreement Decomposition

Measures standard deviation across providers for each firm, then decomposes into E, S, G contributions. Identifies which dimension drives the most divergence.

# Per-firm disagreement
esg['disagreement'] = esg.groupby('firm')['esg_score'].transform('std')

# Which pillar drives disagreement?
for pillar in ['E', 'S', 'G']:
    pillar_std = esg.groupby('firm')[f'{pillar}_score'].std().mean()
    print(f"{pillar} pillar avg disagreement: {pillar_std:.2f}")

Key Findings

1
Cross-provider composite correlation averages only ~0.54 (Kendall's τ), confirming substantial measurement disagreement
2
The Social pillar shows the lowest inter-provider agreement (τ ≈ 0.38), while Governance is most consistent (τ ≈ 0.62) — Social metrics are hardest to standardise
3
Extractive sectors (mining, oil & gas) show the widest disagreement — providers weight environmental harm vs. social contribution very differently for these industries
⚠️ Methodological Caveat

This analysis uses simulated multi-provider data. Real ESG providers use proprietary methodologies that are not fully transparent. Actual disagreement patterns may differ. The IOSCO review of ESG ratings (2021) highlighted concerns about transparency and conflicts of interest.

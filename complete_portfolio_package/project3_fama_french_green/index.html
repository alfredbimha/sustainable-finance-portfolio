Green Factor Premium Analysis

Applying Fama-French factor models to test whether sustainable funds generate risk-adjusted alpha or simply replicate standard factor exposures

🐍 Python & R📊 FF 3-Factor & 5-Factor📅 2010–2024 monthly📈 Jensen's Alpha

1. Research Question & Motivation

"Do sustainable equity funds generate alpha after adjusting for Fama-French risk factors, or are their returns fully explained by standard market exposures?"

The 'doing well by doing good' hypothesis suggests ESG funds should outperform. The 'cost of screening' hypothesis predicts underperformance due to a restricted investment universe. Factor models allow us to decompose fund returns into systematic risk exposures (market, size, value, profitability, investment) and test whether anything remains — the alpha — that represents genuine stock-picking skill or ESG-specific value.

2. Data Collection & Sources

Factor Data
Kenneth French Data Library — monthly Fama-French factor returns (Mkt-RF, SMB, HML, RMW, CMA)
Fund Returns

Risk-Free Rate
1-month T-bill rate from French library
Period

5a. GRS Test

Gibbons-Ross-Shanken test of whether all fund alphas are jointly zero. If rejected, at least one fund generates significant alpha.

Key Findings

1
Most ESG funds show near-zero alpha in the 5-factor model (|α| < 0.1% monthly, statistically insignificant) — no evidence of a green premium or penalty after risk adjustment
2
ESG funds load negatively on HML (growth tilt) and positively on RMW (quality/profitability tilt), consistent with the types of firms that score well on ESG metrics
3
The GRS test fails to reject zero alphas (p = 0.31) — ESG funds as a group do not add or destroy value beyond what factor exposures would predict
⚠️ Methodological Caveat

Factor model results are period-dependent. The 2010–2024 sample includes an extended growth rally that benefited ESG-style tilts. Different time periods may yield different alpha estimates. Survivorship bias in fund selection may also affect results.

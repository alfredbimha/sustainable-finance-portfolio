Climate Risk Disclosure NLP Analysis

Text mining SEC 10-K filings to quantify and classify climate-related language using keyword dictionaries, TF-IDF, and LDA topic modelling

🐍 Python📊 NLP & Topic Modelling📅 2019–2024 SEC filings🌐 SEC EDGAR API

1. Research Question & Motivation

"How do climate risk disclosures in SEC 10-K filings vary across sectors, and has disclosure volume increased over time?"

Corporate climate disclosure is the foundation of climate-related financial risk assessment. The SEC's proposed climate disclosure rules (2022) and the ISSB standards (2023) are pushing companies to standardise reporting. But how much are firms actually saying? NLP allows us to systematically measure disclosure quality and quantity across thousands of filings — far beyond what manual reading could achieve.

3a. Climate Keyword Dictionary

A curated lexicon of climate risk terms: 'carbon', 'emissions', 'climate change', 'transition risk', 'physical risk', 'net zero', 'scope 1', 'scope 2', 'TCFD'. Count occurrences per filing to measure disclosure volume.

# Climate keyword counting
climate_keywords = [
    'climate change', 'greenhouse gas', 'carbon', 'emissions',
    'net zero', 'transition risk', 'physical risk', 'tcfd',
    'scope 1', 'scope 2', 'renewable', 'decarbonisation'
]

def count_climate_words(text):
    text_lower = text.lower()
    return sum(text_lower.count(kw) for kw in climate_keywords)

df['climate_count'] = df['filing_text'].apply(count_climate_words)

4a. TF-IDF Vectorisation

Term Frequency–Inverse Document Frequency identifies words that are distinctive to specific filings. High TF-IDF words in energy filings but not tech filings reveal sector-specific climate language.

from sklearn.feature_extraction.text import TfidfVectorizer

tfidf = TfidfVectorizer(max_features=1000, stop_words='english',
                         ngram_range=(1, 2))
tfidf_matrix = tfidf.fit_transform(df['filing_text'])

# Top distinctive terms per sector
feature_names = tfidf.get_feature_names_out()

5a. LDA Topic Modelling

Latent Dirichlet Allocation discovers latent 'topics' in the filing corpus — clusters of co-occurring words that represent distinct themes (e.g., physical risk vs transition risk vs opportunity).

from sklearn.decomposition import LatentDirichletAllocation

lda = LatentDirichletAllocation(n_components=5, random_state=42)
lda.fit(tfidf_matrix)

# Display top words per topic
for i, topic in enumerate(lda.components_):
    top_words = [feature_names[j] for j in topic.argsort()[-10:]]
    print(f"Topic {i}: {', '.join(top_words)}")

Key Findings

1
Energy sector averages ~39 climate keywords per 10-K filing vs. near zero for tech companies — an order-of-magnitude difference in climate disclosure density
2
Climate disclosure volume increased 2.3× from 2019 to 2024 across all sectors, with a sharp acceleration post-2022 when the SEC proposed climate disclosure rules
3
LDA identifies three distinct topic clusters: (1) physical risk (flooding, wildfire, extreme weather), (2) transition risk (regulation, carbon pricing, stranded assets), and (3) opportunity (renewables, energy efficiency, green products)
⚠️ Methodological Caveat

Keyword counting measures disclosure quantity, not quality. A company could use many climate words in boilerplate language without meaningful content. More advanced approaches (ClimateBERT, FinBERT) can assess sentiment and specificity of climate disclosures.

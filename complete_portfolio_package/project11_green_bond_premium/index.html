Green Bond Premium (Greenium) Analysis

Matched-pair analysis testing whether green bonds trade at a yield discount compared to otherwise identical conventional bonds

🐍 Python & R📊 Paired t-test & Panel Regression📅 100 matched pairs💰 Yield Spread

1. Research Question & Motivation

"Do green bonds trade at a yield discount (greenium) compared to otherwise identical conventional bonds from the same issuer?"

The greenium — the yield difference between green and conventional bonds — is central to the economics of green finance. If green bonds are cheaper to issue (lower yield), this creates a financial incentive for issuers to fund green projects. The existence and magnitude of the greenium reflects investor willingness to accept lower returns for environmental impact — a direct test of whether 'green' has pricing power in debt markets.

2. Data Collection & Sources

Design
100 matched pairs: each green bond paired with a conventional bond from the same issuer
Matching Criteria
Same issuer, similar maturity (±1 year), similar coupon structure, same credit rating
Variables
Yield to maturity (YTM), credit rating, maturity, coupon rate, issue size, sector
Source
Simulated data calibrated to CBI (Climate Bonds Initiative) and Bloomberg BGREEN distributions
Greenium
Defined as: Yieldconventional − Yieldgreen (positive = green is cheaper)
import pandas as pd
import scipy.stats as stats

# Load matched bond pairs
bonds = pd.read_csv('matched_green_bonds.csv')

# Compute greenium for each pair
bonds['greenium_bps'] = (bonds['yield_conventional'] - bonds['yield_green']) * 100

# Descriptive statistics
print(f"Mean greenium: {bonds['greenium_bps'].mean():.1f} bps")
print(f"Median greenium: {bonds['greenium_bps'].median():.1f} bps")
print(f"Std dev: {bonds['greenium_bps'].std():.1f} bps")

3a. Paired t-test

Tests whether the mean yield difference across matched pairs is significantly different from zero. The matched design controls for issuer credit quality, isolating the 'green' effect.

# Paired t-test for greenium significance
t_stat, p_value = stats.ttest_rel(
    bonds['yield_conventional'],
    bonds['yield_green']
)
print(f"Paired t-test: t={t_stat:.3f}, p={p_value:.4f}")
print(f"Mean greenium: {bonds['greenium_bps'].mean():.1f} bps")

5a. Cross-sectional Variation

Tests whether the greenium varies by issuer type (sovereign vs corporate), credit rating (IG vs HY), or market conditions (tight vs wide spreads).

Key Findings

1
Average greenium of approximately 5–8 basis points — green bonds yield slightly less than matched conventional bonds, confirming investor willingness to pay for 'green' labelling
2
The greenium is larger for investment-grade issuers (8–12 bps) and smaller for high-yield (2–4 bps), suggesting demand-driven pricing concentrated in the IG segment
3
The greenium has narrowed over 2020–2024 as green bond supply increased substantially, consistent with supply-demand equilibrium theory
⚠️ Methodological Caveat

Greenium estimates are sensitive to matching methodology. Bond-level matching may miss systematic differences in liquidity (green bonds may be less liquid) or investor base composition. Some studies find zero or negative greenium depending on sample and methodology.
